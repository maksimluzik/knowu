<!-- GROW Coaching -->
<h2 id="grow-coaching">GROW Coaching Plans</h2>
<p>The <strong>GROW model</strong> (Goal, Reality, Options, Will) is one of the most widely used frameworks for professional coaching and development conversations. KnowU adds a powerful dimension: deep, validated personality data that makes every GROW conversation more meaningful and targeted.</p>

<div class="grow-cards">
  <div class="grow-card">
    <div class="grow-card__letter">G</div>
    <h3 class="grow-card__title">Goal</h3>
    <p class="grow-card__desc">Define what the employee wants to achieve. KnowU's personality profile reveals intrinsic motivators — whether someone is driven by Dominance (results), Influence (recognition), Steadiness (stability), or Compliance (quality) — helping set goals that truly resonate.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="grow-card">
    <div class="grow-card__letter">R</div>
    <h3 class="grow-card__title">Reality</h3>
    <p class="grow-card__desc">Explore where things stand today. MBTI, DISC, and Big Five scores give an objective, multi-source baseline. Self-assessment scores versus peer feedback reveal blind spots — the gap between how someone sees themselves and how colleagues experience them.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="grow-card">
    <div class="grow-card__letter">O</div>
    <h3 class="grow-card__title">Options</h3>
    <p class="grow-card__desc">Generate strategies for growth. KnowU's AI highlights specific development areas per trait — so coaching conversations move from generic advice to personalised, evidence-based paths forward aligned with the person's natural style.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="grow-card">
    <div class="grow-card__letter">W</div>
    <h3 class="grow-card__title">Will</h3>
    <p class="grow-card__desc">Commit to action. Growth Tracking lets employees re-assess over time and compare AI evaluations with self-reflections — creating accountability and visible progress. Managers can revisit sessions with updated personality data.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- Glasl Conflict Prevention -->
<h2 id="conflict-prevention">Conflict Prevention: Glasl's Escalation Model</h2>
<p>Friedrich Glasl's model of conflict escalation describes how workplace disagreements can progress through <strong>9 stages</strong> across three phases — from healthy tension to full-scale destructive conflict. The key insight: <strong>early intervention is exponentially more effective</strong> than trying to resolve advanced-stage conflict.</p>

<div class="glasl-info-box">
  <p><strong>The core challenge:</strong> Most conflict escalates not because people are bad actors, but because they lack self-awareness and don't understand how their personality and communication style affects others. KnowU addresses this at the root.</p>
</div>

<div class="glasl-model">
  <div class="glasl-phase glasl-phase--green">
    <div class="glasl-phase__header">
      <span class="glasl-phase__label">Phase 1 — Win / Win</span>
      <span class="glasl-phase__subtitle">Healthy Tension — Keep Teams Here</span>
    </div>
    <div class="glasl-stages">
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--1">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">1</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Hardening</strong>
          <p>Different opinions clash. Positions harden but dialogue remains possible.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">💡 KnowU helps by surfacing DISC &amp; MBTI differences — explaining <em>why</em> two people see things differently.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--2">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">2</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Debates &amp; Polemics</strong>
          <p>Discussions become debates. Each side tries to prove the other wrong.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">💡 Peer feedback data helps both parties see how their communication style lands — before it escalates.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--3">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">3</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Actions Not Words</strong>
          <p>Talking stops working. Actions replace conversation. Empathy decreases.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">💡 Agreeableness scores and moral compass data help managers identify who may be withdrawing — and intervene early.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="glasl-phase glasl-phase--amber">
    <div class="glasl-phase__header">
      <span class="glasl-phase__label">Phase 2 — Win / Lose</span>
      <span class="glasl-phase__subtitle">Destructive Escalation — Urgent Mediation Needed</span>
    </div>
    <div class="glasl-stages">
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--4">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">4</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Images &amp; Coalitions</strong>
          <p>Stereotypes form. Others are recruited to take sides.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">⚠️ Intervention required. Personality data can help a mediator understand each party's perspective and underlying drivers.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--5">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">5</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Loss of Face</strong>
          <p>Public attacks on character and dignity. Trust is severely damaged.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">⚠️ Professional mediation essential. KnowU data supports structured dialogue but cannot resolve trust breakdown alone.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--6">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">6</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Threat Strategies</strong>
          <p>Ultimatums are used. The conflict becomes a power struggle.</p>
          <div class="glasl-stage__knowu">⚠️ HR and leadership must step in. Focus on structural resolution, not personality insights.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="glasl-phase glasl-phase--red">
    <div class="glasl-phase__header">
      <span class="glasl-phase__label">Phase 3 — Lose / Lose</span>
      <span class="glasl-phase__subtitle">Extreme Escalation — External Arbitration Required</span>
    </div>
    <div class="glasl-stages">
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--7">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">7</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Limited Destructive Blows</strong>
          <p>Winning becomes less important than hurting the opponent.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--8">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">8</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Fragmentation</strong>
          <p>Goal is to disintegrate the opponent's system entirely.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="glasl-stage glasl-stage--9">
        <span class="glasl-stage__num">9</span>
        <div class="glasl-stage__content">
          <strong>Together into the Abyss</strong>
          <p>Both parties accept mutual destruction. No resolution possible internally.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
